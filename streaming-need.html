Definition of Streaming

According to Merriam Webster “streaming is an act, the process, or an instance of Streaming data”. so in live streaming, it is an act of recording yourself live on a platform, data being transferred, which can be referred to as the video being streamed to you at an instance

We can find the need for streaming simply by saying its for entertainment but one thing we learned from philosophers is that there's a deeper meaning to the need for streaming, so in order to do this we will have the following, that helps us towards this journey of finding the need in streaming.

Types of Streaming Content


Gaming streams. A type of content that mainly focuses on the streamer or creator playing a game. Podcasts. Podcast is a type of content where the speakers usually but not exclusively film audios. Vodcasts. Unlike podcasts, vodcast is a type of content where the creator film both the audio and video. Webcasts. Webcasts are usually filmed or taped live, including both audio and video. Music Videos. This content type features a video, typically of a newly released song to help with promotion.

Platforms used for Streaming

Twitch, Youtube, Facebook, TikTok, and Linkedin.

Twitch is a very accessible and interactive platform that has a whole culture built within it. Youtube., because of the huge amount of users this platform has, the discoverability of a stream is higher than platforms with less viewers. Facebook streams get recommended to the user's feed, helping the streamer gain viewers. TikTok, the algorithm of the app helps new streamers to get discovered by suggesting their streams to the user's for you page or by redirecting the viewers to another stream after one ended. LinkedIn is a platform previously used professionally but now has started focusing on content creation where you can access videos, articles, and livestreams.

Characteristics of a Good Stream

Good communication skills. Streamers need good communication skills to be able to communicate with their viewers in a natural way. Respectful. They say that the viewers are the same as the streamers. If that's true than a respectful streamer would also be able build a respectful community. Entertaining. A stream should be entertaining, whether that be cracking jokes or showing reactions to things, the viewers need to be entertained. Uniqueness. To be able to stand out, a stream needs to have it's own brand and stick to it. Authenticity. Being real during streams doesn't only help the streamer be comfortable, but it also helps the viewers connect with them more.

Need of Viewers


Entertaining. Viewers should constantly be entertained or they will click out of the creator's content. Engaging. Actively taking opinions from the audience is a great way if engaging and interacting with them. Creative. Be it something that is exclusively your brand or something you do like a catchphrase, the viewer needs to see the creativity in order to pay attention to your content. Intention. If the intention of your content is to create something to out others down, be prejudiced and disrespectful, it's not unlikely that less people will watch you.
