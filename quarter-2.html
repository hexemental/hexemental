EG8-9: Streaming Tools

The group was asked to complete a worksheet for this experience guide. The worksheet was divided into 2 parts: Experience 1 and 2.

See our work
For Experience 1 the question was "Describe the broadcasting software your group chose." The group's answer was "OBS Studio can stream on many platforms such as ( Facebook, Youtube, Twitch, etc.)"

For the second question: List down the reasons for how your group arrived at a decision, the group's answers were as follows:

Obs is more multi-purpose, you can stream or record it
Obs is significantly optimized therefore it can be made to run on a low-end device
Considering how easy it is to install and record, it offers us convenience.
Obs is very known in the streaming community therefore finding tutorials for it is easily accessible whenever needed.

For Experience 2, We were first asked about our brainstorming and to tell it in detail. It started with us casually talking about streaming and our favorite content until we came to the conclusion that devices such as a Laptop or a PC, Microphone, and a webcam are highly recommended when streaming.

EG10: What's your Content

See our work
This EG was the start of the planning for our content. During this EG we were asked about what we brainstormed about in detail. We came up with three contents. The first one was a stream about how to assemble a bike. The second one was a Genshin gaming stream, and the third one was a Podcast.

In the end, we decided to go with the Genshin gaming stream. For the stream we would showcase new updates, and patch notes, and fight the weekly bosses.

After that, we were asked to write down our expectations for the stream. In a nutshell, the group wanted to keep the stream entertaining and gain at least an average of 10 viewers throughout the stream.

EG11: Stream Design

For this EG it was time for us to design our stream layout or what other people call the stream overlays. We settled with Palette #0AD6E1, #00508F, and #FFDE59.

As this is in a slide presentation, we also included the design itself. Our layout was a template from Canva, and we customized it to fit our theme and palette. We added stickers of Genshin characters around the slides. We had a starting screen, the stream layout itself, and an ending screen.

EG12: Guidelines to keep in Mind

For this EG we were asked to list down the DOs and DON'Ts when it comes to Data and Privacy when streaming.

See our work
The DOs are:

Do respect everyone.
To engage with audiences by applying MCH values such as mindfulness and professionalism.

The DON'Ts are:

Do not use profanity
Do not ask viewers for personal information such as phone numbers and addresses.
Do not share personal information with viewers such as phone numbers and addresses.

EG 13: Stream Outline

For this EG we decided on the outline of our stream. The stream would run for about 1 hour and 25 minutes. For the stream, we decided to have 2 activities. For the first activity, we will be clearing weekly bosses. For the second activity, we will tour the map and showcase the update and patch notes.

Time Topic Activity Time Allotment Screen Audio
3:00-3:05 Starting Screen Prepare for the stream 5 mins Video Muted audio

3:55-4:20 Activity 2 Tour Around the Map 25 mins Video/Gameplay Mic/Game Audio
