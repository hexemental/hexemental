EG1: Introduction to Streaming


This group of strangers, strangely, worked well with each other. During the activities of the first lesson where they needed to work together, they were given sets of questions to find out what role in the group fits them most. They found out that Em fits the facilitator role the most. Xander and Mane fit the most as a spokesperson, and Kimi, Raze, and Jay all fit the most as a scribe.

Then, they were asked to start to discuss the things to consider in regard to their content, using the Design Thinking Process. It was kind of a short activity for them; Mane filled up the given diagram and brainstormed with the other members about the contents of it.

While that was being finished, Xander was working on another task. It was a game review of sorts. In a nutshell, he compared the game he played to a PS3 game and praised that the game developers were able to run a game that looked like a PS3 game using just software since PS3 games relied heavily on hardware.

EG2: What are the Different Platforms for Streaming?

See our work
During the activities for the second lesson, Xander contributed the most by doing both the comparison of platforms and the logical operators.
In the end, after answering the checkpoint questions the group concluded a few things.

Twitch is the best option when it comes to streaming since it’s very well-optimized and was designed to work well with various other programs. In connection to this, the web interface was also questioned and its simplicity was deemed important since it’s efficient for the users.

Mobile compatibility was also discussed; for a streamer to do well, one thing that contributes to their performance on the platform is their discoverability. For some countries and people who cannot afford any other device besides a phone, it is crucial for them to be able to hear about or watch a streamer. The accessibility of a platform was also mentioned, explaining that it can both be profitable and efficient for the site’s usability.

Navigation is useful to streamers specifically, as it shows them the streaming time, the number of viewers, and the money earned throughout the stream. In connection to viewers, fast load times are also valued as it enhances user retention on the platform.

Lastly, the importance of Video and Sound quality was discussed. Video quality is important in improving the interactions between the streamer and the audience and the sound quality is debatably more important than the video quality, especially for podcasts, webcasts, and people who have “audio only” mode on; It helps them get immersed in the conversation more.



EG3-4: Who Watches These?

In this lesson, the members reviewed the Comparison and Logical operators they previously accomplished and determined what content type is suitable for different types of audiences. This was also the start of the creation of the groups’ IPBA.

This was integrated with English and focused on the content type appropriate to the research topic.

Of all the content types, the group decided to pick between three types of content: Educational Videos, Podcasts, and Interviews. They concluded that these three types were the most appropriate for their research topic: Teenagers’ Escapism: Why do they do it?

The Audience Analysis was done by Mane and Em. Mane suggested, created, and sorted the responses into the respective demographic information. Em on the other hand worked on the Beliefs and Opinions on the topic, as well as the Attitudes on the topic parts of the analysis.

EG5-6: Why Do People Watch Streams?

In this lesson, they still focused on their IPBA. This time, finding out the reason why people watch streams. They were asked to maximize the different techniques of the empathize stage.

Mane and Em used three different methods. Em used What-How-Why questions, Mane used Analogous Empathy, and both of them worked on the third method: 5 whys.

All of the methods point out the same reasons. Their target demographic watches streams to temporarily escape from reality, cope, and relieve stress. The respondents use different methods, depending on how they deal with stress, and with the survey, it was concluded that the majority of the respondents use online media content to help them feel better.
